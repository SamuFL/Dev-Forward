// why

The industry is changing.
Fundamentally.

Austria has trained a massive number of software developers in recent years — at universities, universities of applied sciences, technical colleges, and bootcamps. Software development has become one of the most reliable career paths, and rightly so. But this very reliability has led many developers to settle into their professional routines — and now they're being caught off guard by the speed of the AI revolution.

The reality: Even though there will be more software in the future, the economy will likely pay fewer developers to build it. Agentic coding, AI assistants, and automated development pipelines are fundamentally reshaping the industry.

At the same time, there's an enormous opportunity. Those who act now — learning new technologies, building projects, experimenting — can help shape the digital transformation rather than being swept away by it. The problem: there's no course for this, no predefined path. But there is a community that can figure it out together.

// core message

Yes, the change is real and serious. But those who act now have the chance to be at the forefront of the new world of software development. Not just individually — Vienna and Austria can take a leadership role if we become active as a community.
